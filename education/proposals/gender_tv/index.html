Bachelor Thesis: Observing gender roles in mainstream TV shows

Observing gender roles in mainstream TV shows. Bachelor Thesis.

Note: This is an exploratory research project.

The power of mass media, such as TV shows, lies in their ability to influence our cultural and political ideas through the lens of their characters or the society they depict. In non-fictional dramas these representations can provide, for the most part and with some limitations, an accurate account of how a particular issue is perceived within a portion of the society. The goal of this project is to study how gender is represented in western TV shows.

This project has two parts:

  1. The modeling of TV scripts (that we will provide) using a combination of networks (a graph structure to represent actors and their interactions) and text annotations;

  2. Study if and how we can infer the gender of the characters based on the interactions between them using a combination of methodologies from network science and statistical inference or machine learning.

The second part of the wok can be extended further based o your interests and the number of students. For example, you can make a comparative study between different countries, across several decades or between TV genres.

This is an exploratory research project - meaning the goals are flexible and open for discussion based on your interests and background.
